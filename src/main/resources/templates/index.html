CA state offers the following schemes and plans:

SNAP

SNAP provides food-purchasing assistance for low- and no-income individuals and families in the U.S. Benefits are distributed via EBT cards, which can be used to buy eligible food items at authorized retailers. The program aims to support adequate nutrition and health.

CCAP

CCAP helps low-income families and those on Temporary Assistance pay for child care, allowing them to work, attend school, or participate in job training. Financial assistance covers some or all child care costs based on family circumstances, with eligibility determined by income and other factors.

Mediaid

Medicaid is a joint federal and state health plan for low-income individuals, providing essential medical coverage. It covers services like doctor visits, hospital stays, and prescription drugs, with eligibility varying by state.

Medicare

Medicare is a federal health plan for citizens aged 65 and older, and for some younger individuals with disabilities. It provides essential medical coverage, including hospital stays, medical services, and prescription drugs.

QHP

A Qualified Health Plan is a commercial health insurance plan that meets the Affordable Care Act standards. It offers comprehensive coverage, including emergency care, hospitalization, and preventive services, and can be purchased through the Health Insurance Marketplace.
